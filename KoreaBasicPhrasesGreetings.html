Korean Greetings

Korean greetings reflect respect, politeness, and social awareness.

Greetings in Korea are more than simple expressions—they show respect, politeness, and social awareness. The choice of greeting depends on the time of day, social relationship, and level of formality.

Common Korean Greetings

Bowing is often used along with verbal greetings.

안녕하세요 (Annyeonghaseyo)

Standard polite greeting meaning “Hello.”
Suitable for most social situations.

안녕 (Annyeong)

Casual greeting used among close friends or younger people.
Can mean “Hi” or “Bye.”

안녕하십니까 (Annyeonghasimnikka)

Very formal and respectful greeting.
Used in speeches, news broadcasts, and formal settings.

좋은 아침입니다 (Joeun achimimnida)

“Good morning” in polite speech.

좋은 저녁입니다 (Joeun jeonyeogimnida)

“Good evening” in polite speech.

만나서 반갑습니다 (Mannaseo bangapseumnida)

“Nice to meet you.”
Polite and formal; used when meeting someone for the first time.

잘 지냈어요? (Jal jinaesseoyo?)

“How have you been?” Polite and friendly.

오랜만이에요 (Oraenmanieyo)

“Long time no see.” Used after not seeing someone for a while.

Cultural Notes

Korean greetings vary by age, relationship, and social hierarchy.

Bowing slightly is customary when greeting, especially in formal situations.
Using polite forms shows respect for age, social rank, and relationship.
Casual greetings are reserved for close friends, family, or younger individuals.
