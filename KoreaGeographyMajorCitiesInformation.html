Major Cities of South Korea

1. Seoul

Seoul is the capital and largest city of South Korea, home to over 9 million people in the city proper and nearly 25 million in the metropolitan region. It is the nation’s center for government, finance, culture, and technology. Seoul blends modern skyscrapers with historic sites like Gyeongbokgung Palace, lively shopping areas such as Myeongdong, and technological hubs like Gangnam.

2. Busan

Busan, located on the southeastern coast, is the country’s second-largest city. Known for its beaches—especially Haeundae and Gwangalli—Busan also hosts the Busan International Film Festival and boasts South Korea’s busiest port. Seafood lovers also flock to Jagalchi Market.

3. Incheon

West of Seoul, Incheon is known for its major international airport, one of the busiest and best-ranked in the world. The city features the futuristic Songdo International Business District, a large port, and cultural landmarks like Korea’s only official Chinatown.

4. Daegu

Daegu, located in the southeast, is recognized for its textile industry and its traditional medicine heritage. Surrounded by mountains, Daegu is a blend of city life with natural scenery. Popular events include the Daegu International Opera Festival and the famous Chimac Festival (chicken + beer).

5. Daejeon

Often called South Korea’s science and technology capital, Daejeon hosts major research centers, government science agencies, and top universities. The city also features attractions like Expo Park and several natural hot springs.

6. Gwangju

Gwangju is known as a major center for art, culture, and democracy in South Korea. It is home to the Gwangju Biennale, one of Asia’s most important contemporary art festivals. The city played a historic role in the 1980 pro-democracy movement, shaping modern Korean political identity.
