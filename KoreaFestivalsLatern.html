Yeondeunghoe (Korean Lantern Festival)

A vibrant celebration of light, peace, and enlightenment.

Yeondeunghoe, the Korean Lantern Festival, is celebrated on the 8th day of the 4th lunar month in honor of Buddha’s Birthday. Known for its bright lanterns and joyful processions, the festival blends spiritual devotion with cultural celebration.

Lantern Parades

Lanterns of all shapes light the streets during massive parades.

Streets come alive with colorful lanterns shaped like animals, flowers, and Buddhist symbols. In Seoul, Jogyesa Temple and Cheonggyecheon Stream are among the most popular locations for the grand parade.

Temple Visits and Ceremonies

Visitors pray for health, wisdom, and peace.

People visit Buddhist temples to pray for blessings, light lanterns, and observe rituals led by monks. Chanting, meditation, and sutra ceremonies mark the spiritual aspect of the festival.

Lighting Lanterns

Lanterns symbolize enlightenment and hope.

Lighting a lantern represents wisdom, hope, and enlightenment. Many visitors write wishes on lanterns before lighting or hanging them, believing the light will carry their hopes into the world.

Cultural Performances

Folk dances, music, and modern light shows brighten the night.

The festival includes traditional music, folk dances, and Buddhist performances. In recent years, modern light shows and interactive installations have brought new energy to the celebration.

Community Participation

Communities unite through lantern-making events.

Schools, local groups, and families often gather to make lanterns together. This spirit of collaboration highlights the festival’s focus on unity, peace, and shared joy.

Cultural Significance

A celebration of spiritual light and social harmony.

Yeondeunghoe symbolizes spiritual awakening, peace, and harmony within the community. It bridges religious devotion with public celebration, making it a cherished event that shines with both cultural and spiritual meaning.
