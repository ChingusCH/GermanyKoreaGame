Sightseeing Highlights

Gyeongbokgung Palace

One of Seoul’s most iconic landmarks, Gyeongbokgung Palace was built during the Joseon Dynasty and remains a major cultural symbol of Korea. Visitors can explore its grand courtyards, intricate palace halls, and the National Folk Museum located within the complex.

Bukchon Hanok Village

Nestled between two royal palaces, Bukchon Hanok Village offers a beautiful collection of traditional Korean houses known as hanok. Many function as tea houses, workshops, or cultural centers, providing a living look into Korean history.

N Seoul Tower (Namsan Tower)

Located atop Namsan Mountain, N Seoul Tower provides sweeping panoramic views of Seoul. The city lights at night make it especially breathtaking. Walking paths and scenic spots around the tower make it popular among tourists and locals alike.

Jeju Island

Famous for its volcanic landscapes and coastal scenery, Jeju Island is one of South Korea’s top destinations. Visitors can hike Hallasan (the country’s highest peak), explore lava tube caves, visit waterfalls, and relax on scenic beaches.

Gyeongju – “The Museum Without Walls”

Once the capital of the ancient Silla Kingdom, Gyeongju is filled with historic treasures. Famous sites include Bulguksa Temple, Seokguram Grotto, and the massive royal burial mounds.

Busan

South Korea’s coastal city Busan combines beaches, culture, and urban life. Haeundae Beach is one of the country’s most famous shorelines, while Gamcheon Culture Village offers colorful houses, murals, and art-filled alleys.
