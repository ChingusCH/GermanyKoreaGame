Seollal (Korean Lunar New Year)

Families gather across Korea to welcome the Lunar New Year.

Seollal is one of the most important traditional holidays in South Korea, celebrated on the first day of the lunar calendar—usually in late January or February. It is a time devoted to family, ancestors, traditions, and fresh beginnings.

Charye (차례) – Ancestral Rites

A carefully prepared ancestral table honors family lineage.

Families perform charye, memorial rituals honoring deceased ancestors. Special foods such as rice cakes, fruits, fish, and traditional dishes are placed on the ritual table. This ceremony reflects gratitude, remembrance, and the continuity of family heritage.

Sebae (세배) – New Year’s Bow

The deep bow symbolizes respect and New Year blessings.

Younger family members perform a deep bow called sebae to elders, saying:
“새해 복 많이 받으세요” (Saehae bok mani badeuseyo)
Meaning: “Wishing you lots of luck in the New Year.”

Elders often give 세뱃돈 (sebaetdon), or New Year’s money, in return as a blessing.

Traditional Clothing – Hanbok (한복)

Bright, elegant hanbok adds beauty to the celebration.

Many Koreans wear traditional hanbok during Seollal for ceremonies and family gatherings. The clothing symbolizes formality, respect, and cultural identity.

Food

Eating tteokguk symbolizes becoming one year older.

A central Seollal dish is tteokguk, a rice cake soup believed to bring good fortune and symbolize aging a year. Families also prepare a wide variety of side dishes to share.

Games and Festivities

Traditional games bring fun to family gatherings.

Families enjoy traditional games such as:
• Yutnori – a four-stick board game
• Kite flying
• Spinning tops

These activities strengthen family bonds and provide joyful celebration.

Gift-Giving and Travel

Millions travel to reunite with their families.

Seollal is one of the busiest travel periods in Korea, as people return to their hometowns. It is common to exchange small gifts such as fruit baskets, sweets, or money.

Cultural Significance

A holiday of gratitude, respect, and renewal.

Seollal emphasizes family unity, respect for elders, cultural continuity, and gratitude. It is a meaningful time for reflection, honoring heritage, and offering hopes for good fortune in the new year.
