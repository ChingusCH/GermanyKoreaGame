Currency & Payments in South Korea

Banknotes and coins in circulation, including ₩1,000 to ₩50,000 bills and ₩1 to ₩500 coins.

Currency

The official currency of South Korea is the South Korean Won (KRW, ₩). Banknotes are available in ₩1,000, ₩5,000, ₩10,000, and ₩50,000 denominations. Coins come in ₩1, ₩5, ₩10, ₩50, ₩100, and ₩500.

Payments

Cash

Cash is widely accepted, especially in traditional markets, street food stalls, and smaller shops.

Credit & Debit Cards

Major cards like Visa, MasterCard, and BC Card are accepted in most cities. Some rural areas or smaller businesses may prefer cash.

Mobile Payments

Popular apps include KakaoPay, Naver Pay, and Samsung Pay. QR code payments are increasingly used in shops, cafes, and public transportation.

Tipping

Tipping is generally not customary in South Korea and is usually unnecessary. Exceptional service in high-end restaurants may sometimes include a service charge.

Tips for Travelers

  • Always check if small shops accept cards or if cash is required.
  • Currency exchange is available at banks, exchange counters, and airports.
  • ATMs supporting international cards are common; look for the Global ATM sign.
