Chuseok (Korean Thanksgiving)

A major harvest festival celebrating abundance and family.

Chuseok is one of South Korea’s most important traditional holidays, celebrated on the 15th day of the 8th lunar month, typically in September or early October. Families gather to honor ancestors, share seasonal foods, and celebrate the autumn harvest.

Charye (차례) – Ancestral Rites

A table of newly harvested foods honors family ancestors.

Families prepare a ritual table filled with rice, fruits, vegetables, and harvest foods to honor their ancestors. This charye ceremony expresses gratitude and respect for the family lineage.

Songpyeon (송편) – Half-Moon Rice Cakes

Handmade songpyeon is a beloved symbol of Chuseok.

Songpyeon are half-moon shaped rice cakes filled with sesame seeds, beans, or chestnuts. Traditionally, they are steamed over pine needles, giving them a unique fragrance. Families often make them together as part of Chuseok celebrations.

Visiting Ancestral Graves (성묘, Seongmyo)

Families clean and honor ancestral graves during Chuseok.

Families visit the graves of ancestors, clean the area, and offer food or bows. This practice, called seongmyo, shows deep respect and gratitude.

Traditional Clothing – Hanbok (한복)

Hanbok adds elegance and tradition to the holiday.

Some families wear hanbok for ancestral rites, family photos, or special gatherings, highlighting cultural pride and tradition.

Games and Cultural Activities

Traditional games and dances bring joy to the holiday.

Popular Chuseok activities include:
• Ssireum (Korean wrestling)
• Yutnori
• Folk dances, songs, and local festivities

These games bring families and communities together in celebration.

Gift-Giving & Food Sharing

Families share feasts and exchange seasonal gifts.

Families prepare large feasts with seasonal dishes such as jeon, galbi, and fresh harvest foods. Exchanging gifts—like fruit boxes, sweets, or household gift sets—is also a common tradition.

Cultural Significance

A holiday of gratitude, unity, and tradition.

Chuseok highlights family unity, ancestral respect, and gratitude for a bountiful harvest. It is a cherished time when families honor heritage, share stories, and strengthen generational bonds.
