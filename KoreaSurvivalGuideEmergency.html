Emergency Numbers in South Korea

Key phone numbers to call in different types of emergencies.

Knowing emergency numbers in South Korea is essential whether you're visiting or living in the country. Most emergency lines are free from any phone (including mobiles). When calling, speak clearly and give your exact location.

Key Emergency Numbers

112
Police — theft, assault, public-safety emergencies

119
Fire & Medical Emergencies — fires, ambulance, rescue

1339
Medical information & assistance — medical guidance & non-emergency health advice

1345
Consumer Protection Hotline — fraud, scams, consumer complaints

120
Local Government / Municipal Information — civil inquiries and guidance

Practical Tips

  • Call first, then explain: provide your exact address or nearest landmark, describe the situation, and give your phone number.
  • Language support: many emergency centers can provide basic English assistance, but speaking clearly and slowly helps.
  • Useful Korean phrases:
    “도와주세요!” (Dowajuseyo!) — “Please help me!”
    “응급 상황입니다.” (Eunggeup sanghwang-imnida) — “It’s an emergency.”
  • Rural areas: be especially specific about landmarks or GPS coordinates, as addresses can be less precise.
  • From landlines or mobile phones: dialing 112 or 119 is free on both types of phones.

Other Resources

For non-urgent medical questions or to find nearby hospitals, call 1339. For consumer issues such as fraud, contact 1345.

When Abroad

If you are visiting from overseas, keep your embassy or consulate's contact information handy in case you need consular assistance. (Embassy phone numbers vary by country — store them in your phone before travel.)
