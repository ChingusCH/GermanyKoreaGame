Asking for Help in Korean

Politeness and respect are key when requesting help in Korea.

Knowing how to ask for help politely is essential in Korean culture, where respect and courtesy guide social interactions. Your phrasing depends on the situation, the level of formality, and your relationship with the person.

Common Phrases

Different situations require different levels of politeness.

도와주세요
(Dowajuseyo)

“Please help me.”
Polite and commonly used in everyday situations.

도와주실 수 있나요?
(Dowajusil su innayo?)

“Could you help me?”
Slightly more formal; often used with strangers or in public places.

죄송하지만, 도와주실 수 있나요?
(Joesonghajiman, dowajusil su innayo?)

“Excuse me, could you help me?”
Very polite—combines an apology and a request, showing humility.

잠깐만 도와줄래?
(Jamkkanman dowajullae?)

“Can you help me for a moment?”
Casual—used with close friends or people your age.

이거 어떻게 해요?
(Igeo eotteoke haeyo?)

“How do you do this?”
Polite way to ask for instructions or guidance.

무슨 뜻이에요?
(Museun tteus-ieyo?)

“What does this mean?”
Polite question used when seeking clarification.

Cultural Notes

A small bow can enhance politeness when asking for assistance.

• Always begin politely, especially with strangers, elders, or in formal settings.
• Adding 죄송하지만 (“Excuse me / I’m sorry”) softens your request.
• A slight bow while asking for help shows additional respect.
• Casual endings like -줄래? should be used only with friends or younger people.
