Traditional Clothing: Hanbok

Hanbok is Korea's traditional clothing, known for its vibrant colors, elegant lines, and flowing shapes. Once worn daily, it is now used for celebrations, festivals, weddings, and cultural events. Hanbok reflects core Korean values of harmony, beauty, and tradition.

Design & Components

Hanbok is designed to flow naturally with the body, without tight-fitting elements.

  • Jeogori: Jacket worn by both men and women
  • Chima: High-waisted, full skirt (women)
  • Baji: Loose-fitting pants (men)
  • Durumagi: Overcoat for warmth or formality

Colors & Symbolism

Hanbok colors traditionally followed the obangsaek philosophy:

  • Blue — East
  • Red — South
  • Yellow — Center
  • White — West
  • Black — North

Bright colors were reserved for youth, while muted tones represented maturity.

Occasions

Hanbok is commonly worn for:

  • Chuseok (Korean Thanksgiving)
  • Seollal (Lunar New Year)
  • Weddings & doljanchi (first birthdays)
  • Ceremonies, cultural events, and performances

Modern Hanbok

Today, modern designers blend tradition with comfort—using lighter fabrics, softer colors, and simplified shapes. Modern hanbok is often worn casually or in urban fashion.

Cultural Significance

Hanbok represents Korea’s identity, history, and artistry. Its smooth, flowing silhouette symbolizes grace, while the colors and structure reflect centuries of Korean philosophy and aesthetics.
