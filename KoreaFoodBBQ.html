Korean BBQ (Gogigui)

Example: diners grilling meat at the table, surrounded by side dishes.

Korean BBQ, known as Gogigui (literally “meat roasting”), is one of the most popular aspects of Korean cuisine. It is a social dining experience where diners cook various types of meat, typically beef, pork, or chicken, at the table on built-in grills.

Types of Meat

Bulgogi, Galbi, Samgyeopsal, and Dak-galbi are popular options.

Bulgogi: Thinly sliced marinated beef, often sweet and savory.
Galbi: Marinated beef short ribs, grilled over charcoal or gas.
Samgyeopsal: Thick, unsalted pork belly slices, usually grilled without marinade.
Dak-galbi: Spicy marinated chicken, sometimes cooked with vegetables.

How It’s Served

Banchan, or side dishes, accompany the main grilled meat.

Korean BBQ is often served with a variety of banchan, small side dishes including:

  • Kimchi (fermented vegetables)
  • Pickled radishes and cucumbers
  • Bean sprouts, spinach, or seasoned vegetables
  • Lettuce or perilla leaves for wrapping the grilled meat
The typical way to eat Korean BBQ is to place a piece of grilled meat on a lettuce leaf, add garlic, sauces, or kimchi, wrap it up, and eat in one bite. This method, called ssam, emphasizes communal sharing and flavor combination.

Dining Experience

Interactive grilling encourages conversation and group participation.

Korean BBQ is highly interactive. Diners often grill their own meat at the table, which encourages conversation and group participation. Restaurants may provide built-in grills or portable tabletop grills for home gatherings.

Cultural Significance

Korean BBQ emphasizes sharing and community.

Korean BBQ reflects Korea’s emphasis on community and sharing food. It is popular for family meals, gatherings with friends, business dinners, and celebrations. Beyond Korea, it has become a global culinary trend, with Korean BBQ restaurants found worldwide.
