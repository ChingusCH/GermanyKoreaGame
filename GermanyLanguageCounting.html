Numbers and Counting in German

Numbers are essential in German for telling time, stating prices, giving addresses, and counting objects. German number patterns are logical, but compound numbers above twenty can be challenging because the unit comes before the tens.

Basic Numbers (0–10)

0 – null
1 – eins
2 – zwei
3 – drei
4 – vier
5 – fünf
6 – sechs
7 – sieben
8 – acht
9 – neun
10 – zehn

Numbers 11–19

Formed using a base number + -zehn.

11 – elf
12 – zwölf
13 – dreizehn

Tens (20–50)

20 – zwanzig
30 – dreißig
40 – vierzig
50 – fünfzig

Compound Numbers (21–99)

In German, the unit comes first, then “und,” then the tens.

21 – einundzwanzig
42 – zweiundvierzig

Hundreds, Thousands, Millions

100 – hundert
1,000 – tausend
1,000,000 – eine Million

Cultural Significance

Knowing numbers is essential for shopping, travel, banking, reading signs, and everyday life in Germany.
