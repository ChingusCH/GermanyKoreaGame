Currency and Payment in Germany

Germany uses the Euro (€) as its official currency. Understanding how to handle money and payments is important for traveling, shopping, and daily life. Both cash and electronic payments are commonly used.

Currency

Euro (€): Coins range from 1 cent to 2 euros, and banknotes range from 5 to 500 euros.

Small coins are frequently used for exact change, and rounding is common in everyday transactions.

Payment Methods

Cash (Bargeld): Still widely used, especially in small shops, bakeries, and markets.

Debit/Credit Cards: Commonly accepted, though some places prefer EC (debit) cards.

Mobile Payment: Apple Pay and Google Pay are becoming increasingly popular.

Tipping

Rounding up the bill or adding around 5–10% in restaurants is standard. Tips are usually given directly to the server.

Cultural Significance

Understanding payment customs in Germany helps ensure smooth transactions and reflects respect for local practices.
