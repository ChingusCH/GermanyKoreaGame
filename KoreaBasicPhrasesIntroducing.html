Introducing Yourself in Korean

Introductions in Korea reflect politeness and respect.

Introducing yourself in Korea is an important part of social etiquette. The way you introduce yourself depends on the formality of the situation and the people involved.

Basic Self-Introduction Phrases

Politeness levels change depending on social context.

안녕하세요, 저는 [Name]입니다.
(Annyeonghaseyo, jeoneun [Name] imnida.)

“Hello, I am [Name].”
A polite, standard introduction used in most situations.

제 이름은 [Name]입니다.
(Je ireumeun [Name] imnida.)

“My name is [Name].”
Another polite and formal phrasing.

만나서 반갑습니다.
(Mannaseo bangapseumnida.)

“Nice to meet you.”
Commonly follows your name in formal introductions.

[Country/City]에서 왔습니다.
([Country/City]-eseo wasseumnida.)

“I am from [Country/City].”
A polite way to share your background.

저는 [Occupation/School]에서 일합니다/다녀요.
(Jeoneun [Occupation/School]-eseo ilhamnida/danyeoyo.)

“I work/study at [place].”
Useful in professional or school-related settings.

안녕, 나는 [Name]야.
(Annyeong, naneun [Name]-ya.)

Casual introduction used only with close friends or younger people.

Cultural Notes

A small bow adds respect to your introduction.

Using polite forms such as 입니다, 합니다, and 다녀요 is essential when meeting strangers or elders.

A slight bow while introducing yourself is customary, especially in formal contexts.

Sharing details like nationality, job, or even hobbies is common but optional.
