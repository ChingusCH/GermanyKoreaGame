The German Alphabet

The German alphabet is based on the Latin alphabet and consists of 26 standard letters, the same as English, plus four special characters: ä, ö, ü, and ß. These letters are essential for correct spelling, pronunciation, and reading in German. Learning the alphabet is the first step in understanding the language.

Special Letters in German

Ä, Ö, Ü (Umlauts)
These vowels change pronunciation and can change meaning.
Example: schon (already) vs. schön (beautiful).

ß (Eszett or sharp S)
Represents a double “s” sound.
Example: Straße (street).

Pronunciation Notes

Most German letters are similar to English, but some differ in pronunciation. For example, V is usually pronounced like “f”, and W is pronounced like “v”.

Cultural Significance

Mastering the German alphabet is key for reading, writing, and pronunciation. It is also essential for understanding German signs, documents, and everyday communication.
